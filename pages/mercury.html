Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Its orbital period around the Sun of 87.97 days is the shortest of all the planets in the Solar System. Mercury has no natural satellites and is the most cratered planet in our solar system.

Astrology

In astrology, Mercury represents communication, intellect, and adaptability. Known as the "Messenger of the Gods," Mercury governs thinking, learning, writing, and all forms of expression. It's associated with the zodiac signs Gemini and Virgo, embodying qualities of curiosity, versatility, and analytical thinking. Mercury represents our mental processes and how we communicate with the world around us.

Alchemy

Within alchemical traditions, Mercury symbolizes the principle of volatility and the element of Mercury (quicksilver). It represents the process of dissolution, where matter is broken down into its most basic form. Mercury embodies the principle of transformation and adaptability, representing the alchemical stage where consciousness becomes fluid and capable of taking any form or shape.

Astronomy

Mercury is a terrestrial planet with a heavily cratered surface similar to Earth's Moon. It has no atmosphere to speak of and experiences extreme temperature variations, from -180°C to 430°C. Mercury has no moons and its surface is dominated by impact craters, smooth plains, and long, steep cliffs called scarps.
