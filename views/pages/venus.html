Venus

Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and mass. Named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, Venus is the brightest natural object in Earth's night sky after the Moon. This terrestrial planet has a thick atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide, creating a runaway greenhouse effect that makes Venus the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures reaching 900째F (475째C).

Astrology

In astrology, Venus represents love, beauty, harmony, and values. Known as the "Planet of Love and Beauty," Venus governs our relationships, artistic sensibilities, and what we find attractive and valuable. It's associated with the zodiac signs Taurus and Libra, embodying qualities of grace, charm, diplomacy, and the pursuit of pleasure and harmony. Venus represents our capacity for love, our aesthetic preferences, and our ability to create beauty and maintain harmonious relationships with others.

Alchemy

Within alchemical traditions, Venus symbolizes the principle of conjunction and the element of Copper in its most refined and beautiful form. It represents the process of union and harmony, where opposites come together to create something greater than their individual parts. Venus embodies the principle of transformation through love and represents the alchemical stage where consciousness becomes refined and capable of perceiving beauty, creating harmony, and achieving balance between the material and spiritual aspects of existence.

Astronomy

Venus is a terrestrial planet with a diameter of about 7,521 miles (12,104 kilometers), making it nearly the same size as Earth. Its surface is hidden beneath a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide (96.5%) with clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a perpetual overcast that reflects about 70% of incoming sunlight. Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction to most planets (retrograde rotation), with one Venusian day lasting 243 Earth days, longer than its year of 225 Earth days.

Venus's Surface Features

Venus has no moons, but its surface features are fascinating and unique. The planet's surface is dominated by vast plains covered in volcanic rock, with numerous volcanoes, impact craters, and mountain ranges. The highest point on Venus is Maxwell Montes, which rises about 7 miles (11 kilometers) above the average surface level. Venus also has numerous coronae (circular features formed by volcanic activity) and tesserae (highly deformed terrain), indicating a geologically active past.
