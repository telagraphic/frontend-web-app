Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in our Solar System. This ice giant, named after the Roman god of the sea, has a deep blue appearance caused by methane in its atmosphere. Located approximately 2.8 billion miles from Earth, Neptune completes one orbit around the Sun every 165 Earth years, meaning it has only completed one full orbit since its discovery in 1846.

Astrology

In astrology, Neptune represents spirituality, dreams, illusions, and transcendence. Known as the "Planet of Dreams and Mysticism," Neptune governs our connection to the divine, artistic inspiration, and the dissolution of boundaries. It's associated with the zodiac sign Pisces, embodying qualities of compassion, intuition, and spiritual awareness. Neptune represents our capacity for unconditional love, creativity, and the ability to transcend material reality through imagination and faith.

Alchemy

Within alchemical traditions, Neptune symbolizes the principle of dissolution and the element of Water. It represents the process of purification through dissolution, where matter is broken down to reveal its spiritual essence. Neptune embodies the principle of transformation through surrender and represents the alchemical stage where consciousness becomes fluid and capable of merging with the universal ocean of consciousness, transcending individual identity.

Astronomy

Neptune is an ice giant with a diameter of about 30,775 miles (49,528 kilometers), making it the fourth-largest planet by diameter and the third-largest by mass. Its atmosphere is composed primarily of hydrogen (80%) and helium (19%), with trace amounts of methane that give it its distinctive blue color. Neptune has the most active weather system in the solar system, with wind speeds reaching up to 1,200 miles per hour (2,100 km/h) - the fastest planetary winds known.

Neptune's Moons

Neptune has 14 known moons, each with unique characteristics and fascinating origins. The largest and most significant is Triton, which was discovered just 17 days after Neptune itself. Triton is unique among large moons as it orbits in a retrograde direction, suggesting it was captured from the Kuiper Belt.
